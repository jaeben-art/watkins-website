Frequently Asked Questions

Booking

How can I book one of your trips?

You can book by clicking the Book Now button on any trip detail page. If you need help, you can also contact us by phone, text, email, or through the Contact Us page.

Can you help me find a roommate?

Roommate matching is not available. To receive double-occupancy pricing, trips must be booked as a pair. If one person cancels, the remaining traveler is responsible for single-occupancy pricing, finding a replacement, or canceling as well.

Is there an age limit?

Some trips include casino or winery stops, so all travelers must be 21 or older.

Payments & Insurance

What payment methods do you accept?

Check is preferred. Checks for bus tours are payable to Diamond Tours and mailed to Watkins Shared Adventures, 215 N 4th St, Cannelton, IN 47520. Other arrangements may be available on request. The purchaser is responsible for all processing and service fees, and any payment processing fees are non-refundable.

What is your cancellation policy?

Diamond Tours cancellation policy applies. If cancellation is submitted at least two weeks before departure, a full refund is issued. Refunds are typically processed within 7 to 10 business days.

Is travel insurance available?

Yes. Diamond Tours offers travel insurance through Travel Confident. Insurance can be purchased directly from their website using the tour ID listed in trip details.

After Booking

Where does the motorcoach pick up?

The primary pickup location is the Tell City Walmart, 730 U.S. Hwy 66, Tell City, IN 47586. Additional pickup locations are limited and not guaranteed. Any pickup location must be along the trip route. Contact us to discuss options.

When will I receive trip details?

Final itineraries are usually available about 30 days before departure. You will receive itinerary details, luggage tags, and any meal selection requests at that time.

Can you accommodate mobility needs?

Some trips are better suited than others for travelers with mobility concerns. Mobility devices can often be accommodated, but staff cannot provide significant physical assistance due to liability limits.
